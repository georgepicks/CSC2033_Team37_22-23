About Us

We are a dedicated group of Computer Science students from Newcastle University, committed to making a positive difference in the world. Our project, "Leftovers", is aimed at tackling one of the most pressing challenges of our time - hunger.

Aligned with the United Nations' goal of Zero Hunger, our web application provides a platform for sharing leftovers. Our goal is to reduce food waste and ensure that no one in our community goes to bed hungry.

Users can register as suppliers, enabling them to post their spare food for others to use. Alternatively, users can register as consumers, allowing them to access these leftovers free of charge. By doing this, we hope to create a cycle of sharing and caring in our community.

Why It Matters

Around the world, over 690 million people suffer from hunger, and 1.3 billion tonnes of food is wasted every year. These numbers are staggering, but we believe that with small steps, we can make a big difference. By reducing food waste and sharing what we have, we can work towards a future where everyone has enough to eat.
